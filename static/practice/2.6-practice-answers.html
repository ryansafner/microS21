2.6 — Long Run Industry Equilibrium — Practice Problems

Solutions

ECON 306 - Fall 2020

The supply of bicycle rentals in a small town is given by:

\[q_S=10p-200\]

1. Find the inverse supply function.

Solve the supply function for \(p\):

\[\begin{aligned} q_S&=10p-200 & & \text{The supply function}\\ q_S-10p&=-200 & & \text{Subtracting }10p\\ -10p&=-200-q_S & & \text{Subtracting }q_S\\ p &=20+\frac{1}{10}q_S & & \text{Dividing by }-10\\ \end{aligned}\]

2. What is the price elasticity of supply at a price of $25.00?

First we use the supply function and find \(q_S\) when \(p=25\):

\[\begin{aligned} q_S&=10p-200 & & \text{The supply function}\\ q_S&=10(25)-200 & & \text{Plugging in 25}\\ q_S&=250-200 & & \text{Multiplying}\\ q_S&=50 & & \text{Subtracting}\\ \end{aligned}\]

Now we know the price, quantity, and slope, so we can use the elasticity formula.

\[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_S &=\frac{1}{slope} \times \frac{p}{q_S} & & \text{The formula for elasticity}\\ \epsilon_S &=\cfrac{1}{\frac{1}{10}} \times \frac{25}{50} & & \text{Plugging in}\\ \epsilon_S &=10 \times 0.5 & & \text{Simplifying}\\ \epsilon_S &=5 & & \text{Multiplying}\\ \end{aligned}\]

This is relatively elastic.

3. What is the price elasticity of supply at a price of $50.00?

First we use the supply function and find \(q_S\) when \(p=50\):

\[\begin{aligned} q_S&=10p-200 & & \text{The supply function}\\ q_S&=10(50)-200 & & \text{Plugging in 25}\\ q_S&=500-200 & & \text{Multiplying}\\ q_S&=300 & & \text{Subtracting}\\ \end{aligned}\]

Now we know the price, quantity, and slope, so we can use the elasticity formula.

\[\begin{aligned} \epsilon_S &=\frac{1}{slope} \times \frac{p}{q_S} & & \text{The formula for elasticity}\\ \epsilon_S &=\cfrac{1}{\frac{1}{10}} \times \frac{50}{300} & & \text{Plugging in}\\ \epsilon_S &=10 \times \frac{50}{300} & & \text{Simplifying}\\ \epsilon_S &=1.667 & & \text{Multiplying}\\ \end{aligned}\]

This is also relatively elastic, but less elastic than at the lower price.
